The Akan people of Ghana frequently name their children after the day of the week they were born and the order in which they were born.

These "day names" have furthur meanings concerning the soul and character of the person.

Middle names have considerably more variety and can refer to their birth order, twin status, or an ancestor's middle name.

Most Ghananians have at least one name from this system even if they also have and English or Christian name.

Notable figures with day names include Ghana's first president Kwameh Nkurumah and former United Nations Secretary-General Kofi Annan.
